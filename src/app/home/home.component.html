Each year 13.5 lakh people are killed on roadways all over the world, out of this 2.7 lakh accidents ( 20% ) are caused by sleepiness, fatigue, and drowsy driving.

The problem of drowsy driving is compounded by the fact that it can be difficult to detect. Unlike driving under the influence of alcohol or drugs, there is no simple test to determine whether a driver is too tired to drive safely.

Additionally, many people underestimate the effects of fatigue on their driving abilities, which can lead them to continue driving even when they are too tired.

Meet my creators

Our solution to the problem of drowsy driving is to use AI sleep detection technology (OpenEye) to monitor drivers and detect signs of drowsiness.

OpenEye uses sensors, and algorithms, and analyze the driver's behavior, such as eye movements, head position, and steering patterns, to determine whether the driver is drowsy or asleep.

When OpenEye detects signs of drowsiness, it alerts the driver with a warning signal, such as a sound or vibration, to encourage the driver to take a break or switch drivers.
